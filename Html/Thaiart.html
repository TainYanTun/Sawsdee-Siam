Traditional Thai Murals

Thai mural paintings adorn Buddhist temples and palaces across Thailand, depicting scenes from Buddhist cosmology, the Jataka tales, and Thai history. These intricate artworks showcase the skill of Thai artisans through their detailed compositions and vibrant colors.

Wat Phra Kaew Murals

The walls of Wat Phra Kaew in Bangkok feature exquisite murals depicting the Ramakien, the Thai version of the Ramayana epic, with over 178 panels telling the complete story.

Traditional Techniques

Thai muralists use natural pigments from minerals and plants, combined with gold leaf, applying paint on dry plaster in the secco technique that has been used for centuries.

Northern Style Murals

Wat Phra Singh in Chiang Mai showcases the distinctive Lanna style of mural painting with its unique color palette and depiction of local daily life alongside religious themes.
